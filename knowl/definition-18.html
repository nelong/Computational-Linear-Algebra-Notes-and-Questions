Definition 3.6.1.

A vector space, \(V\text{,}\) is a nonempty set of objects called vectors with two operations called addition and scalar multiplication such that the following hold for all \(\vec{u}, \vec{v}, \vec{w} \in V\) and \(c,d \in \mathbb{R}\text{:}\)
  1. If \(\vec{u}, \vec{v} \in V\text{,}\) then \(\vec{u}+\vec{v}\in V\text{.}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \vec{u}+\vec{v}=\vec{v}+\vec{u}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle (\vec{u}+\vec{v})+\vec{w}=\vec{u}+(\vec{v}+\vec{w})\)
  4. There exists a vector \(\vec{0}_V\) such that \(\vec{v}+\vec{0}_V=\vec{v}\text{.}\)
  5. For each \(\vec{u} \in V\text{,}\) there is a vector \(-\vec{u} \in V\) such that \(\vec{u} + (-\vec{u})=\vec{0}_V\text{.}\)
  6. If \(\vec{u} \in V\) and \(c \in \mathbb{R}\text{,}\) then \(c\vec{u} \in V\text{.}\)
  7. \(\displaystyle c(\vec{u}+\vec{v})=c\vec{u}+c\vec{v}\)
  8. \(\displaystyle (c+d)\vec{v}=c\vec{v}+d\vec{v}\)
  9. \(\displaystyle c(d\vec{v})=(cd)\vec{v}\)
  10. \(\displaystyle 1 \vec{v}=\vec{v}\)
You can refer to these properties as
  1. closure of vector addition
  2. commutativity of vector addition
  3. associativity of vector addition
  4. existence of the zero vector
  5. existence of the additive inverse
  6. closure of scalar multiplication
  7. distributive property of scalar multiplication across vector addition
  8. distributive property of scalar addition across scalar multiplication (of a vector)
  9. associativity of scalar multiplication
  10. existence of scalar multiplicative identity

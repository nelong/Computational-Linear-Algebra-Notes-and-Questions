Exploration 2.5.2.

The explanations for the next tasks may be difficult for you to write but will be very helpful in ensuring you understand the vector calculus concepts of this chapter. This is a continuation of ExplorationÂ 2.5.1 and all of the same constraints still apply.

(a)

Describe how the value of \(a_{\vec{N}}\) would be felt by the racecar driver in our analogy.

(b)

In terms of our race car situation, explain why \(a_{\vec{N}}\) can't be negative.

(c)

Suppose that at time \(t=b\text{,}\) Jane is at a location which we will denote \(P_1\) in her race car. The velocity and acceleration vectors for Jane are \(\langle{2,0,-2}\rangle\) and \(\langle{3,2,-3}\rangle\text{.}\) Calculate the following:
  1. \(\displaystyle \vec{T}(b)\)
  2. \(\displaystyle a_{\vec{T}}(b)\)
  3. \(\displaystyle a_{\vec{N}}(b)\)
  4. \(\displaystyle \vec{N}(b)\)
  5. \(\displaystyle \kappa(b))\)

(d)

Suppose that at time \(t=c\text{,}\) Nick is at a location \(P_1\) in his race car. Without any calculation at all, answer the following:
  1. \(\displaystyle \vec{T}(c)\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \vec{N}(c)\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \kappa(c))\)

(e)

The velocity and acceleration vectors for Nick (at time \(c\)) are \(\langle{1,0,-1}\rangle\) and \(\langle{-1,2,1}\rangle\text{.}\) Calculate the following:
  1. \(\displaystyle a_{\vec{T}}(c)\)
  2. \(\displaystyle a_{\vec{N}}(c)\)

(f)

Is Jane or Nick turning the steering wheel farther? Explain how you know this based on your calculations above.

(g)

Is Jane using the gas pedal or the brake pedal when she is at \(P_1\text{?}\) Explain how you know this based on your calculations above.

(h)

Is Nick using the gas pedal or the brake pedal when he is at \(P_1\text{?}\) Explain how you know this based on your calculations above.

(i)

Nick and Jane talk to thier friend Jeremy who claims that his velocity and acceleration vectors at position \(P_1\) are \(\langle{4,0,6}\rangle\) and \(\langle{8,9,10}\rangle\text{.}\) Explain how Jane and Nick know he is lying.

We want to find a matrix \(C\) such that \(AC=Id\text{.}\) So let's expand \(C\) as columns.
\begin{equation*} AC=A [\vec{c}_1 \; \vec{c}_2 \; \ldots \; \vec{c}_n]=[A\vec{c}_1 \; A\vec{c}_2 \; \ldots \; A\vec{c}_n] \end{equation*}
Using this perspective on the equation \(AC=Id\text{,}\) we get
\begin{equation*} A\vec{c}_1=\colvec{1 \\ 0\\0\\\vdots\\0}, \; A\vec{c}_2=\colvec{0 \\ 1\\0\\\vdots\\0}, \: \cdots, \; A\vec{c}_n=\colvec{0 \\ 0\\0\\\vdots\\1} \end{equation*}
How would we find out if there were any solutions to these matrix equations?

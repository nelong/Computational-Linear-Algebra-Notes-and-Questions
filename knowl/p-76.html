In this example, we will list out ALL of the possible reduced row echelon forms of a two by two matrix. We will use the symbols \(\blacksquare\) for a non-zero entry and \(*\) for an entry that can be any real number. We will also use \(0\) and \(1\) for entries that must be \(0\) and \(1\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 0 \\0 \amp 1 \end{bmatrix} , \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp * \\0 \amp 0 \end{bmatrix} , \begin{bmatrix} 0 \amp 1 \\0 \amp 0 \end{bmatrix} , \begin{bmatrix} 0 \amp 0 \\0 \amp 0 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
You should think carefully to see if there are any possibilities missing here.

    Section 1.2 Systems of Linear Equations and Matrices

    Objectives

    • To understand reliable and efficent ways of finding solutions to systems of linear equations
    • To understand the terms and methods associated with matrices corresponding to linear systems of equations

    Subsection 1.2.1 Linear systems of equations

    Question 1.2.1.

    How would you solve the following system?
    \begin{gather*} x+y=4\\ 2x-y=2 \end{gather*}
    Your answer to the previous problem suggests (at least) two more questions.
    • How do we interpret solutions? What is the meaning of a solution?
    • Is there always a solution to linear system of equations?

    Question 1.2.2.

    (a)
    How would you solve the following system?
    \begin{gather*} x+y=4\\ 3x+3y=7 \end{gather*}
    (b)
    How would you solve the following system?
    \begin{gather*} x+y=4\\ 3x+3y=12 \end{gather*}
    (c)
    What possibilities do you think are possible for the set of solutions to a linear system?

    Subsection 1.2.2 Matrix Forms of Linear Systems of Equations

    Consider the linear system with \(m\) equations in \(n\) unknowns:
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rcrcrcrcr} a_{11} x_1 \amp +\amp a_{12} x_2 \amp +\amp ... \amp +\amp a_{1n}x_n \amp =\amp b_1 \\ % -1 3 a_{21} x_1 \amp +\amp a_{22} x_2 \amp +\amp ... \amp +\amp a_{2n}x_n \amp =\amp b_2 \\ \vdots \amp \amp \vdots \amp \amp \amp \amp \vdots \amp \amp \vdots \\ a_{m1} x_1 \amp +\amp a_{m2} x_2 \amp +\amp ... \amp +\amp a_{mn}x_n \amp =\amp b_m \end{array} \end{equation*}
    The rectangular array of coefficents \(a_{ij}\) given as
    \begin{equation*} A \equiv \begin{bmatrix} a_{11} \amp a_{12} \amp ... \amp a_{1n} \\ a_{21}\amp a_{22}\amp ... \amp a_{2n} \\ \vdots \amp \vdots \amp \amp \vdots \\ a_{m1}\amp a_{m2} \amp ... \amp a_{mn} \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
    is called the coefficent matrix.

    Question 1.2.3.

    For each system given, write the coefficent matrix.
    (a)
    \begin{align*} 3x_1-2x_2\amp =6\\ 2x_1-2x_2\amp =-2\\ -x_1+x_2\amp =1 \end{align*}
    (b)
    \begin{align*} 3x_1-2x_2-4x_3\amp =6 \end{align*}
    (c)
    \begin{align*} 3x_1-2x_2\amp =6\\ 2x_1-2x_2\amp=6\\ -x_1+x_2\amp =1 \end{align*}
    (d)
    \begin{align*} x\amp =5\\ 4x\amp =-1\\ -2x\amp =10 \end{align*}
    (e)
    \begin{align*} 7x\amp =3\\ 2y\amp =1\\ 3z\amp =-2 \end{align*}
    (f)
    \begin{align*} 3r-5s+t\amp =2\\ -6r+10s-2t\amp =3 \end{align*}

    Section 2.4 The TNB Frame of Motion in 3D

    Objectives

    • To understand how the unit tangent, unit normal, and binormal vectors are measured for a curve in space
    • To understand how to efficently calculate the unit tangent, unit normal, and binormal vectors in terms of a parameteriation

    Question 2.4.4.

    Mark each of the following as either TRUE or FALSE. If they are false, explain why they are false.
    1. If a particle travels at constant speed, its acceleration vector is \(\vec 0\text{.}\)
    2. If a particle travels at constant speed, its path must be a straight line.
    3. If a particle is moving along a parameterized curve, then the acceleration vector at a point cannot be parallel to the velocity vector.
    4. The arc length of a parameterized curve depends on the parameterization used to compute the integral.
    5. If a particle is moving around a circle, then its acceleration and velocity vectors are orthogonal.
